Catalog Description & Materials

Catalogue Course Description This course presents knowledge of organism, population, and community biology. It is the second semester of a two-semester sequence (BIO150 & BIO151). Prerequisites: Successful completion of BIO150 or instructor approval.
Required Textbook/ISBN This course uses a course fee model from McGraw-Hill Higher Education. This means that you will have access to the Connect platform that includes an eBook and all assignments without purchasing any additional materials. If you prefer a physical copy of the textbook it is available through the SCC bookstore.

Course Competencies

  1. Describe the structure and function of living organisms.
  2. Demonstrate knowledge of the classification of living organisms.
  3. Analyze relationships between organisms and their environment.
  4. Describe evolution, including natural selection, as the unifying theory of life.
