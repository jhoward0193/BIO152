Use of AI Tools

“In this course AI is like a microscope, in practicing biology it can reveal patterns and prompt better questions, but you do the observing, interpreting, and explaining.”

AI tools (e.g., ChatGPT, Copilot, Grammarly) may be used to support learning, not replace your own analysis and writing.

  • Disclose any AI assistance with a brief note at the end of your submission (e.g., “Used ChatGPT to brainstorm headings; I revised all text”).
  • Verify & cite: You are responsible for accuracy and proper citation. Do not use fabricated sources/citations.
  • Original work: Submitting unedited AI text as your own may constitute academic dishonesty.
  • Privacy: Do not upload exam prompts, unpublished data, or classmates’ work to public tools.

The instructor may require drafts/process evidence or an oral check. Suspected misuse will be handled under college academic integrity policies.
